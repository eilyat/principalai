Ruyi is an Economist at Principal Economics, holding a Master of Commerce in Economics, specialising in economic modelling and data analysis. With a robust foundation in statistical software and a deep interest in energy economics, Ruyi is adept at analysing and interpreting complex economic data relevant to energy policies and market trends.

Drawing on experiences from various consulting and research roles, Ruyi has honed a keen acumen for assessing the economic impacts of energy-related projects and policies. Her expertise spans across evaluation frameworks, essential for high-level energy economics analysis and client engagement. 
Ruyi is currently in the process of publishing her master's dissertation on renewable energy, as well as a research project on housing unaffordability.
